Trip Plan

This itinerary mixes Tokyo's modern energy, Osaka's food-and-nightlife culture, and Kyoto's historic streets and temples.

  1. Tokyo

    Big-city highlights, famous neighborhoods, digital art, and a Mt. Fuji day trip.

    • See Shibuya Crossing and head up to Shibuya Sky for a panoramic view of the city.
    • Explore Shinjuku at night for the lights, crowds, and nonstop atmosphere.
    • Visit teamLab Borderless for a fully immersive digital art museum experience.
    • Take a day trip to Mt. Fuji for one of the most iconic views in Japan.
    • Walk through Harajuku and Takeshita Street for style, shopping, and energy.
    • Visit Meiji Shrine and spend time around the Asakusa temple area to balance the modern side of Tokyo with traditional culture.

  2. Osaka

    Street food, bright neon, castle views, big-city energy, and a meaningful day trip to Hiroshima.

    • Spend time in Dotonbori for the street food, neon lights, and signature Osaka energy.
    • Try local favorites like takoyaki and okonomiyaki.
    • Visit Osaka Castle for one of the city's best-known historic landmarks.
    • Go shopping in Shinsaibashi and explore one of Osaka's busiest districts.
    • Take a day trip to Hiroshima from Osaka for an important historical experience during the trip.
    • Lean into Osaka's nightlife and food scene at night.

  3. Kyoto

    Traditional streets, temple landmarks, peaceful walks, and day trips to Nara and Kibune.

    • Walk through Fushimi Inari Shrine and its famous torii gates.
    • Visit the Arashiyama bamboo forest for one of Kyoto's most recognizable landscapes.
    • Explore the Gion district and Kyoto's old streets for the historic atmosphere I want most from this trip.
    • See Kinkaku-ji, the Golden Pavilion, and one of Kyoto's most beautiful temple sites.
    • Spend time on the Philosopher's Path walking or biking through a quieter side of the city.
    • Take a day trip to Nara to visit the deer park and enjoy another classic cultural stop.
    • Take a day trip to Kibune for a river hike and a quieter mountain setting outside the city.

What Makes This Trip So Good

Tokyo gives us the huge city moments I have always wanted to experience, from Shibuya and Shinjuku to immersive digital art and temple stops that show a completely different side of the city.

Osaka feels like the food-and-nightlife chapter of the trip, while Kyoto is the part that matches the old-street, shrine, and historic atmosphere I picture when I think about Japan.
